Biography

Nikola Tesla



Nikola Tesla was an inventor, physicist, mechanical engineer, and electrician who was born in Smiljan, the Austrian Empire (now Croatia) July 10, 1856. He was the fourth of five children. His father named Milutin Tesla was an Orthodox Priest and his mother, Duka Tesla who had the talent to make home craft tools, mechanical equipment and the ability to memorize Serbian epic poems. Born and raised in the Empire of Austria, Tesla completed his education in engineering and physics in the 1870s. He had worked in the field of telephony in the 1880s and at Edison Continental in the field of electricity industry. He moved to the United States in 1884 and later he was naturalized as a citizen. Nikola Tesla had worked at the Edison Mechine Works in New York City before deciding to leave. With the help of partners to finance and market his ideas, Tesla set up a laboratory in New York to develop a variety of electrical and mechanical equipment. Its alternating current and reverse polyphase patent are licensed by Westinghouse Electric in 1888.
